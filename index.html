01 — The Struggle

What We're Fighting

After successfully forcing the government to abandon a new Terminal 4 at CDG, environmental associations and residents' collectives now face a harder battle. ADP is pushing infrastructure investments through separate administrative procedures — new boarding halls, a freight hub — that collectively represent the same expansion under a different name.

344 additional flights per day by 2050. No public debate. No cumulative environmental assessment.

The strategic problem: Incremental expansion is harder to fight than a single mega-project. ADP is betting that breaking it into pieces will break the coalition too.

Key insight: Both interviews point to the same vulnerability — the public doesn't connect individual projects to the 2050 total. The 344 flights/day figure is buried in corporate roadmaps. Our strategy must make that connection visceral and undeniable.
03 — The Problematic

Fragmentation
as a Weapon

We are not fighting a terminal. We are fighting a logic: that growth is inevitable, that infrastructure can be approved piece by piece without accounting for the whole, and that the people living closest to the noise have the least power over the decision.

Our problematic: How do we make the cumulative, invisible impact of airport expansion legible — and outrageable — to people who don't yet feel personally affected?

The moment someone in Paris's 10th understands that their nephew in Sarcelles sleeps with 72 dB of engine noise, and that ADP's plan adds 344 more flights over that same house — that's when coalitions grow.
04 — Campaign Strategy

Three Phases

1
Make the Invisible Visible — Months 1–3
Launch the GROUND CDG impact calculator. Door-to-door in affected communes (Goussainville, Sarcelles, Villeneuve-le-Roi) to gather real noise testimonies mapped to postcodes. Publish an open data map of projected noise corridors under the 2050 plan. Goal: 10,000 personalized impact cards shared on social media.
2
Build the Coalition — Months 3–6
Connect residents' collectives with climate groups (XR, ANV-COP21), legal teams, and local MPs. Organize a "Night of 344 Flights" — a real-time sound installation in Paris where speakers replay CDG noise for the exact duration of the extra daily flights. Present the legal fragmentation argument to sympathetic parliamentarians.
3
Legal + Political Pressure — Months 6–18
File a legal challenge demanding a cumulative environmental impact assessment for all planned ADP investments. Deliver the petition to the Ministère de la Transition Écologique and the Autorité environnementale. Push for CDG expansion to be included in France's ZFE (low-emission zone) national debate.

06 — The Petition

Formal Demand

For a Cumulative Environmental Assessment of CDG Expansion

To: The Minister of Ecological Transition, the Autorité environnementale, and the Board of Aéroports de Paris

We, the undersigned residents, environmental organizations, and citizens, call for an immediate halt to all infrastructure investment decisions at Roissy-CDG pending a full, transparent, and independent cumulative environmental impact assessment.

ADP's strategy — approving new boarding halls, freight infrastructure, and capacity increases through separate administrative procedures — bypasses the spirit of France's environmental law. These projects are not independent: they serve a single objective of +19% traffic growth by 2050, meaning 344 additional flights over our communities every day.

We stopped Terminal 4. We will not accept the same expansion delivered in fragments.

  • A legally binding cumulative environmental impact assessment covering all planned CDG infrastructure investments and their 2050 traffic targets, conducted by an independent body.
  • A public inquiry specifically addressing noise pollution, air quality, and public health for all communes within the 65 dB Lden isophone.
  • Alignment of ADP's infrastructure roadmap with France's obligations under the Paris Agreement and the national ZFE framework.
  • No construction permits for new boarding halls or freight terminals until assessments are complete and publicly debated.
  • Binding caps on annual flight movements at CDG — not growth targets — as the baseline for any future planning permission.

The sky is not a corporate resource. The health of 3.6 million people under these flight paths is not a line in an infrastructure budget.

07 — Additional Digital Tactics

The Full Toolkit

01
The Cumulative Map

Open-source embeddable map: every planned ADP project overlaid with noise isophones, air quality models, and school/hospital locations. Built on OpenStreetMap + public ADP filings. Updated as permits are filed.

02
The 344 Alarm

A browser notification that fires once per "extra flight" throughout the day — 344 interruptions, calibrated to real ADP scheduling data. You can't ignore 344 disruptions. That's the point.

03
Shareholder Proxy Tool

France's Caisse des Dépôts holds 33% of ADP. A tool generating a pre-written shareholder concern letter any citizen can sign and send directly to CDC — pressure through ownership, not just the streets.

04
Sound Archive

Crowdsourced noise recordings from residents, tagged with GPS and dB levels. Forms a legal-quality sonic archive submitted as supporting evidence in the cumulative impact assessment demand.
